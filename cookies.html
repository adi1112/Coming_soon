Cookie Policy

Effective: 8 November 2025 • Entity: Fern Technologies Ltd trading as “HiveReply”
This Cookie Policy explains what cookies and similar technologies we use, why we use them, and how you can control them. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service.

1) What are cookies and similar tech?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by your browser. We also use similar technologies like localStorage and sessionStorage for preferences, and (on some public pages) lightweight pixels solely for attribution or security.

2) How we use cookies (aligned with our Privacy & Terms)

  • Essential — Required for core functionality and security (authentication/session, CSRF, rate limiting, load balancing, WAF/CDN bot protection like Cloudflare Turnstile). These cannot be switched off.
  • Preferences — Remember your choices (e.g., theme, language, density). Typically stored via first-party cookies or localStorage in the app.
  • Analytics — Privacy-respecting product analytics (aggregated/de-identified). We prefer cookieless measurement; if a cookie is used, it’s first-party and limited in scope.
  • Marketing/Attribution (public site only) — Limited measurement of campaign effectiveness on marketing pages. We do not use third-party advertising cookies for cross-site behavioral ads, and we do not sell or “share” personal information for such advertising.

3) Surfaces

  • Marketing site (www): Essential + optional analytics/attribution.
  • Logged-in app (agent/admin): Essential + minimal product analytics; no advertising cookies.

4) Do Not Track / Global Privacy Control

Industry support for DNT is inconsistent; we do not change behavior based solely on DNT signals. We do not sell or “share” personal information for cross-context ads, and we treat recognized Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals on public pages as an opt-out of any such “sale/share” (which we don’t do).

5) Managing cookies

You can control cookies via your browser settings (block, delete, or limit). Blocking essential cookies may break parts of the site or app. If you’ve implemented a preferences page, visit /cookie-preferences (optional).
